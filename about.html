A short story of a girl from Nairobi

My name is Niceta (Nee-se-ta not nice-e-ta) Nduku (Nn-du-ko).
I have broken down my story into parts just to give a better context of me. There is obviously a lot more than there is on this page. You can navigate through the rest of website to see

Past

When I was 7, I wanted to be a mathematician. It later became math professor because that sounded fancier. Long story short, I have always a nerd. I loved math and science (the basic story of every person in a STEM degree). I was a child who enjoyed school and learning. Passing was not my main motivation, and this is a trait that has stuck with me. I love to understand and figure out... why?
After maths professor, it was architect, orthopaedic surgeon (very short lived), mechatronics engineer (the longest), chemical engineer(also very brief), civil engineer, back to mechatronics then I finally settled on Electrical and Computer Engineer. At some point people stopped asking and to this very day, I have friends who do not know what I am studying. Each of these choices had a motivation behind them. Unlike many African people my age or older, my family had no influence on my choice. Something always inspired me. I do not want to use the most clich√© phrase 'I wanted to solve problems' but it was the truth. Besides my deep interest in the subject, I want to use what I have to make an impact.

present

I decided to run away from home and go south of my home to study engineering at the University of Cape Town. It has been a long and painful journey. No one properly warned me of its difficulty- though they tried. However, I have learnt so much and grown in ways that I did not imagine.
